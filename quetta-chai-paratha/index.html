Our Story

From the mountain passes of Quetta to a corner of Tuwaiq.

In the high country around Quetta, the nights are cold and the chai is serious business. Brewed dark with full-cream milk, green tea, cardamom, and sugar reduced until the kettle sings — that's doodh patti, the cup that fuels long drives, longer conversations, and the kind of dhabas that never close.

We brought that same kettle to Tuwaiq. It stays on, round the clock. Truckers, night-shift workers, students after midnight — the door is open and the chai is hot, every hour of every day.

But the paratha tawa lights up only at dawn. Hand-rolled, layered, slapped onto searing iron with a slick of oil — qeema, chicken tikka, aloo, plain with white butter. Breakfast hours only. The way it's meant to be.

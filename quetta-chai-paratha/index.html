The Story

A slice of Quetta, rolled out in Riyadh

Quetta's chai culture runs on two things: a pot that never goes cold, and dough that gets worked by hand until it's soft enough to fold. This kitchen keeps both traditions alive for Riyadh's Pakistani community and anyone else who's discovered it — regulars say the flavour reminds them of a mother's kitchen back in Lahore or Quetta.

The doors don't close. Chai, tea-time snacks and the daily menu run around the clock, which makes it as much a 3 a.m. stop as a breakfast spot. Staff are known for being warm and quick, and the dining area is kept noticeably clean — small things regulars mention again and again.

It's still a young kitchen, growing its menu one dish at a time — so a little patience (and an early visit for paratha) goes a long way.

On the Tawa

What to order

The paratha list is short and changing as the kitchen grows — these are the dishes regulars keep coming back for.

House Favourite

Qeema Paratha

Spiced minced-meat folded straight into the dough, griddled until the edges crisp up. The dish reviewers mention most.

Classic

Chicken Tikka Paratha

Smoky, well-seasoned chicken tikka wrapped into a hot paratha — a full breakfast in one hand.

Comfort

Chicken Cheese Paratha

Melted cheese and chicken, a milder option for anyone easing into the menu.

All Day

Doodh Patti Karak Chai

Strong, milky, and simmered slow — the pot that never stops boiling, served morning, noon or 3 a.m.

Simple

Plain & Aloo Paratha

The everyday version — buttery, flaky, and best with a side of yoghurt or pickle.

Heads Up
